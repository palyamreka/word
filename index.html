WordPress is a popular open-source content management system (CMS) used for building and managing websites. It was first released in 2003 by Matt Mullenweg and Mike Little and has since become one of the most widely used CMS platforms in the world.

Here’s a comprehensive overview:

### Core Features

1. *Ease of Use*:

WordPress is known for its user-friendly interface, making it accessible for both beginners and experienced users.

2. *Themes*:

Users can choose from thousands of free and premium themes to customize the appearance of their sites.

3. *Plugins*:

A vast library of plugins extends functionality, allowing users to add features such as contact forms, SEO tools, and e-commerce capabilities.

4. *Customizable*:

Offers extensive customization options through themes, plugins, and custom code.

5. *SEO-Friendly*:

Built-in features and plugins help with search engine optimization (SEO).

6. *Mobile-Responsive*:

Many themes are designed to be responsive, ensuring that sites look good on mobile devices.

### Technical Aspects

1. *Open Source*:

WordPress is released under the GNU General Public License (GPL), which means it’s free to use and modify.

2. *PHP and MySQL*:

It is built using PHP and uses MySQL (or MariaDB) for its database management. Overall, WordPress’s flexibility, ease of use, and extensive ecosystem make it a popular choice for a wide range of website projects.
